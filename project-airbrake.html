The Challenge

In high-powered rocketry competitions like AIAA IREC, hitting the target apogee (30,000 ft) precisely is crucial for scoring. Atmospheric variability makes this difficult with passive fins alone. We needed an active drag system capable of real-time adjustments during supersonic flight.

The Solution

I designed a 3-fin actuation system driven by a single high-torque servo. The mechanism utilizes a custom linkage geometry that converts rotational servo motion into linear extension of drag plates.

The assembly was machined from 6061-T6 aluminum to withstand aerodynamic forces up to 200N per fin, verified through static FEA and dynamic airflow simulations.

Results

The system successfully integrated with the avionics bay and passed initial ground actuation tests. Wind tunnel testing confirmed drag coefficient variations matched our theoretical models within 5%.
